ABSTRACT

Integrated Learning Project, also known as ILP, is a learning process or method that combines multiple different subjects into one big project. This system is required for all 9th-grade students in Santa Ursula Junior High School throughout the academic year. For this semester, 6 different groups were assigned to create and plan a bazaar, with each group consisting of 6 people. Different themes, names, and designs for each booth were chosen by us, as well as everything included in our stall, such as products and promotions. Through this whole process of the researchers’ bazaar project, we managed to learn many important skills such as critical thinking, problem-solving, and teamwork, which will be helpful to overcome future challenges and future careers.

The first step to prepare our bazaar was to think of a name and logo. Java Jaws was the name chosen by our group. Java Jaws consists of two words, one of which is "Java" which was taken from the theme of the bazaar, namely Javanese culture. "Jaws" was taken from our leopard mascot, an animal endemic to Java which has the character of courage and integrity. Our leopard mascot was also named Rana, a name that means bravery and courage. After we came up with a solid identity for our booth, we started planning out what we were going to sell. A total of 9 products were sold by us, some made according to the instructions of specific subjects’ assignments. Our products consisted of an aquarium water ring toss toy, chicken popcorn, sticker sheets of our design, perfume as their biotechnology product, a traditional Javanese drink called wedang uwuh, kue cubit, tote bags using our own as well, and a syrup with sprite drink. This was the specific menu chosen by us because we felt that it would be interesting to our customers and fun to make. Our products were then promoted by us through social media platforms using engaging posts and videos leading up to the day of the bazaar. On the day of the bazaar, all of our products were sold out thankfully, but our totebag performed slightly slower than the other products.

One of the challenges that had to be faced throughout the preparation process was finding the right time to complete all our tasks. It was difficult to match up all our schedules to meet up or prepare the products together because everyone was always busy with other activities of their own, so most times we could not meet up all together outside of school hours. Because of this complication, any meetups together had to be planned a week or more before the deadline to ensure everyone was available. Thankfully, since me and my group spent lots of time together, we eventually cleared up that problem by working together and communicating a lot. Other than that, another challenge we faced was adapting to all the new applications and programs we had to use to complete assignments such as CSS and creating many detailed reports because sometimes it was complicated to find the information and data for those things and it would take up lots of time. We eventually did get used to it however, by asking for help from our teachers or to each other.

In conclusion, ILP gave us an unforgettable experience through this bazaar project. We managed to learn many new things such as critical thinking, entrepreneurship, financial management, and more importantly, how to cooperate and communicate as a team. Lots of reports and data tables had to be made, but all challenges were eventually overcome as a team, which I think is a skill that will be very beneficial in the future.
